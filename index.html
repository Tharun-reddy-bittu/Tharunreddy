Butter chicken

Prep Time: 30 minutes

Cook Time: 1 hour

Ingredients

  • 500g chicken breast or thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. : Marinating the Chicken:In a bowl, combine yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, and salt. Add chicken pieces to the marinade and mix well to coat. Allow it to marinate for at least 30 minutes, preferably longer, in the refrigerator.
  2. : Cooking the Chicken: Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add marinated chicken pieces and cook until they are browned and cooked through. Set aside
  3. : Preparing the Sauce: In the same pan, add butter. Once melted, add chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for another minute. Stir in pureed tomatoes and cook until the oil separates from the mixture. Add red chili powder, turmeric powder, ground coriander, ground cumin, garam masala, and salt. Cook for a few minutes until the spices are well incorporated. Pour in heavy cream and mix well. Allow the sauce to simmer for a few minutes .
  4. :Combining Chicken and Sauce: Add the cooked chicken pieces to the sauce and stir well to coat the chicken pieces with the sauce. Allow it to simmer for a few more minutes to let the flavors meld together.
  5. : Serving: Garnish with fresh cilantro leaves. Serve hot with naan, rice, or your choice of bread.

chicken shawarma

Prep Time: 30 minutes

Cook Time: 1-2 hour

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs (about 900g) boneless, skinless chicken thighs, thinly sliced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 teaspoons ground cumin, 2 teaspoons ground paprika
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric, 1 teaspoon ground coriander, 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cloves, 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
  • 1/4 cup (60ml) lemon juice, 1/4 cup (60ml) olive oil
  • Salt and black pepper to taste, Pita bread or flatbreads
  • Optional toppings: sliced tomatoes, cucumbers, lettuce, tahini sauce, garlic sauce, pickles, etc

Instructions

  1. Marinate the Chicken: In a large bowl, combine minced garlic, ground cumin, paprika, turmeric, coriander, cinnamon, cloves, cayenne pepper, lemon juice, olive oil, salt, and black pepper. Add the sliced chicken thighs to the marinade and toss until evenly coated. Cover the bowl and marinate in the refrigerator for at least 1 hour, or ideally overnight for more flavor.
  2. Preheat the Oven or Grill: Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C) or preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  3. Skewer the Chicken (Optional): If using skewers, thread the marinated chicken slices onto skewers. This step is optional; you can also cook the chicken directly on a grill pan or baking sheet.
  4. Cook the Chicken: If using skewers, place them on the preheated grill or in the oven. Cook for about 15-20 minutes, turning occasionally, until the chicken is cooked through and nicely browned. If cooking without skewers, you can place the chicken directly on a grill pan or baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Cook for the same amount of time, turning halfway through..
  5. Rest and Slice: Once cooked, remove the chicken from the grill or oven and let it rest for a few minutes. Then, thinly slice the chicken into bite-sized pieces.
  6. Assemble the Shawarma: Warm your pita bread or flatbreads briefly in the oven or on the grill. Place some sliced chicken down the center of each pita or flatbread. Add any desired toppings, such as sliced tomatoes, cucumbers, lettuce, tahini sauce, garlic sauce, pickles, etc.
  7. Roll and Serve: Roll up the pita bread or flatbread tightly around the filling to form a wrap or sandwich. Serve immediately while warm.
  8. Enjoy: Enjoy your homemade chicken shawarma!

Mutton Haleem

Prep Time: 30 minutes

Cook Time: 3-4 hour

Ingredients

  • 1 cup whole wheat grains (broken wheat) 1/2 cup barley 1/2 cup split Bengal gram (chana dal) 1/2 cup split red lentils (masoor dal) 1/2 cup split pigeon peas (toor dal) 500 grams boneless mutton , cut into small pieces 1 large onion, thinly sliced 4-5 cloves garlic, minced 1-inch piece of ginger, grated 2-3 green chilies, chopped (adjust to taste) 2 tomatoes, chopped 2 tablespoons ghee or vegetable oil 1 teaspoon cumin seeds 1 teaspoon turmeric powder 1 tablespoon red chili powder (adjust to taste) 2 teaspoons garam masala powder Salt to taste Fresh cilantro leaves, chopped, for garnish Lemon wedges, for serving

Instructions

    1. Rinse the whole wheat grains, barley, and lentils under cold water until the water runs clear. Soak them in water for at least 2 hours or overnight.
    2. In a large pot or pressure cooker, add the soaked grains and lentils along with 6-7 cups of water. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer until the grains and lentils are cooked and tender. This may take about 1-2 hours, depending on the type of grains used.
    3. Once the grains and lentils are cooked, use an immersion blender or a potato masher to mash them to a thick, porridge-like consistency. Set aside.
    4. In another large pot or skillet, heat ghee or oil over medium heat. Add the sliced onions and sauté until they turn golden brown and caramelized.
    5. Add the minced garlic, grated ginger, and chopped green chilies to the pot. Sauté for another 2-3 minutes until aromatic.
    6. Add the chopped tomatoes to the pot and cook until they turn soft and mushy.
    7. Add the mutton or chicken pieces to the pot along with turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt to taste. Cook until the meat is browned on all sides.
    8. Add the mashed grains and lentils to the pot and mix well with the meat and spices.
    9. Add enough water to achieve the desired consistency. The haleem should have a thick, stew-like consistency. Simmer for another 30-40 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the flavors are well combined and the haleem reaches the desired thickness.
    10. Sprinkle garam masala powder over the haleem and mix well. Adjust the seasoning if necessary.
    11. Serve hot garnished with fresh cilantro leaves and lemon wedges on the side. Enjoy your delicious haleem!

chicken majestic

Prep Time: 30 minutes

Cook Time: 2 hour

Ingredients

  • 500 grams boneless chicken, cut into thinly pieces
  • 2 tablespoons yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons cornflour
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1 egg
  • Oil for deep frying
  • Curry leaves for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, lemon juice, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, and salt to taste. Mix well to form a smooth paste.
  2. Add the chicken pieces to the marinade and mix until the chicken is well coated. Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 1 hour, or overnight for best results.
  3. Dip each marinated chicken piece in the beaten egg, then coat it with the flour mixture, shaking off any excess.
  4. Carefully drop the coated chicken pieces into the hot oil, in batches, and fry until they turn golden brown and crispy, about 5-6 minutes.
  5. Use a slotted spoon to remove the fried chicken pieces from the oil and drain them on paper towels to remove excess oil.
  6. In a separate pan, heat a little oil and add curry leaves and sliced green chilies. Fry for a few seconds until fragrant.
  7. Garnish the fried chicken with the fried curry leaves and green chilies.
  8. Serve hot as an appetizer or snack, garnished with lemon wedges and onion rings. Enjoy your homemade Chicken majestic! Adjust the spice levels according to your taste preferences
